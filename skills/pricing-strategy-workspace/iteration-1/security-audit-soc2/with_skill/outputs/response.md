# Pricing Recommendation: Full Security Audit + SOC 2 Compliance Preparation

## Client Profile

- **Client type**: Series B fintech startup
- **Funding**: $30M raised
- **Headcount**: ~80 employees
- **Engagement**: Full security audit + SOC 2 Type II compliance preparation
- **Duration**: 3 months
- **Provider**: Cybersecurity consulting firm (first time pricing this exact combination)

---

## Context Assessment

This is a mid-market B2B services engagement with several features that matter for pricing:

- The client is a **fintech company**, which means regulatory and compliance pressures are real and growing. SOC 2 is likely not optional -- it is a prerequisite for enterprise sales, partnerships, and in some cases continued operation.
- **Series B with $30M raised** signals they have budget. They are investing in growth infrastructure, not bootstrapping. Compliance spending is expected at this stage.
- **80 employees** puts them in the range where SOC 2 preparation is complex enough to need external help (multiple teams, systems, processes to audit) but not so large that they have a dedicated compliance team already.
- **3-month engagement** is realistic for a combined security audit + SOC 2 readiness project, assuming moderate existing maturity. If their security posture is immature, 3 months may be tight.
- **First time pricing this exact combination** means we need to be careful about assumptions and build the price from fundamentals rather than pattern-matching to a past engagement.

## Lens 2: Market Analysis

### Comparable Market Rates

Security audit + SOC 2 preparation is a well-established service category. Market rates for this type of engagement vary by firm reputation, scope depth, and geography.

**Security audit only (comprehensive, mid-market fintech)**:
- Boutique firms: $30,000-$75,000
- Mid-tier specialized firms: $60,000-$150,000
- Big 4 / large consultancies: $150,000-$300,000+

**SOC 2 readiness / preparation only (80-person company)**:
- Compliance-focused firms: $30,000-$60,000
- Full-service security firms: $50,000-$100,000
- Big 4: $100,000-$250,000

**Combined security audit + SOC 2 preparation (3 months, fintech)**:
- Boutique / small specialized: $80,000-$150,000
- Mid-tier specialized (most comparable to your positioning): $150,000-$250,000
- Premium / Big 4: $250,000-$400,000+

### Market Context

- Fintech companies at Series B are accustomed to paying for compliance. It is a cost of doing business that directly enables revenue (enterprise sales require SOC 2).
- The cybersecurity consulting market has tightened since 2023-2024, with demand for SOC 2 readiness consistently high as more startups pursue enterprise customers.
- Companies with $30M in funding typically budget 5-15% of annual operating costs for security and compliance. For an 80-person company burning $8-12M/year, that is $400K-$1.8M annually for security. A $200K engagement is within normal range.
- Geographic rate variation: major tech hubs (SF, NYC, Boston) command premium rates. Adjust downward 10-20% for secondary markets.

**Market lens conclusion**: The competitive range for a mid-tier specialized firm delivering this combined engagement is $150,000-$250,000. Premium positioning or additional depth can push above $250,000.

## Lens 3: Value Analysis

### What is this worth to the client?

**Direct value -- enabling enterprise sales**:
- SOC 2 compliance is frequently a hard gate for enterprise fintech deals. Without it, the client cannot close contracts with banks, large financial institutions, or enterprise clients with security requirements.
- If SOC 2 readiness enables even 2-3 enterprise deals in the next 12 months, and average enterprise deal size in fintech is $100K-$500K ARR, the engagement unlocks $200K-$1.5M in revenue.
- At 10-20% of unlocked value, the engagement is worth $20K-$300K to the client from enterprise sales alone.

**Direct value -- breach prevention**:
- The average cost of a data breach for a financial services company is approximately $5.9M (IBM Cost of a Data Breach Report, 2024 figures). For a startup, even a smaller breach could cost $500K-$2M in direct costs plus reputational damage.
- A thorough security audit that identifies and remediates critical vulnerabilities directly reduces this risk.
- If the audit reduces breach probability by even 10-20%, the expected value reduction is $50K-$400K.

**Direct value -- funding and partnerships**:
- Investors at Series C and beyond increasingly require evidence of security maturity. SOC 2 readiness strengthens the company's position for future fundraising.
- Partner integrations (banking APIs, payment processors) often require SOC 2 compliance.

**Indirect value -- operational efficiency**:
- SOC 2 preparation forces process maturation: access controls, change management, incident response, vendor management. These improvements have lasting operational benefits.
- Avoiding the cost of doing this in-house. Hiring a full-time CISO costs $200K-$350K/year plus benefits. A compliance analyst adds another $100K-$150K. This engagement replaces 6+ months of hiring, onboarding, and execution.

### Value Summary

| Value Driver | Estimated Value to Client |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| Enterprise deal enablement (12 months) | $200K-$1.5M |
| Breach risk reduction | $50K-$400K |
| Fundraising / partnership readiness | Hard to quantify, but material |
| Avoided cost of in-house build | $150K-$250K (6-month equivalent) |
| **Total estimated value** | **$400K-$2M+** |

**Value lens conclusion**: At 10-15% of value created, the engagement justifies a price of $40K-$300K. The mid-range of credible value-based pricing is $150K-$250K, which aligns well with market rates. For a fintech client where SOC 2 directly unlocks revenue, pricing toward the higher end of this range is defensible.

## Lens 4: Risk Analysis

### Identified Risks

| Risk Factor | Severity | Description |
|-------------|----------|-------------|
| Scope ambiguity | **Moderate-High** | "Full security audit" and "SOC 2 preparation" can mean very different things to different clients. Without a detailed scope document, the engagement could expand significantly. How many systems, applications, and third-party integrations are in play? Is penetration testing included? Is cloud infrastructure (AWS/GCP/Azure) in scope? |
| Client security maturity | **Moderate-High** | If the client's current security posture is immature (no existing policies, no access controls, no incident response plan), SOC 2 preparation will require building from scratch, not just documenting and tuning. This dramatically increases effort. |
| Client dependencies | **Moderate** | SOC 2 readiness requires heavy client participation: interviews, evidence gathering, policy adoption, remediation implementation. If the client is slow to respond or key personnel are unavailable, the timeline slips and your team idles. |
| Fintech regulatory complexity | **Moderate** | Fintech environments often involve payment processing, PII handling, and financial data subject to additional regulatory requirements beyond SOC 2. Scope creep into PCI-DSS, state money transmitter requirements, or banking partner audits is a real risk. |
| 3-month timeline pressure | **Moderate** | 3 months is achievable but leaves little buffer for discovery surprises, client delays, or remediation complexity. If the client expects SOC 2 audit-readiness (not just preparation) in 3 months, that is aggressive. |
| Post-engagement support | **Low-Moderate** | The client will likely need ongoing support during the actual SOC 2 audit (performed by the auditor, not the consultant). If this is expected but not scoped, it becomes unpaid work. |
| First-time engagement risk | **Low-Moderate** | You have not priced this exact combination before. There is inherent uncertainty in your own cost estimates. |

### Risk Adjustment

Based on the above, this engagement carries **moderate-to-high overall risk**.

Recommended risk adjustment: **+15-25%** on top of the base price.

Rationale:
- Scope ambiguity is the largest risk. A detailed scope document before signing would reduce this, but pricing should account for the ambiguity that exists at proposal time.
- Client maturity is unknown. The adjustment covers the range between "decent starting point" and "building from scratch."
- Timeline pressure means less room to absorb surprises without additional cost.

### Suggested Pricing Structure

**Recommended: Phased engagement with setup fee + monthly retainer**

This structure is better than a flat project fee because it:
- Reduces client sticker shock (monthly feels smaller than one lump sum)
- Creates natural checkpoints to adjust scope
- Protects you against scope creep by defining phase boundaries
- Gives the client an off-ramp if they need one (reduces buyer risk)

**Structure (at target price of $265,000):**

| Component | Amount | Covers |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Phase 1: Discovery and Assessment** (Month 1) | $75,000 | Security audit, gap analysis, current-state documentation, SOC 2 readiness assessment, risk prioritization, remediation roadmap |
| **Phase 2: Remediation and Policy Development** (Month 2) | $105,000 | Policy creation/updates, control implementation guidance, evidence collection framework, remediation support, tool configuration |
| **Phase 3: Audit Preparation and Readiness** (Month 3) | $85,000 | Pre-audit testing, evidence package assembly, auditor liaison preparation, readiness validation, team training |

**Payment terms**: 50% of each phase due at phase start, 50% at phase completion. Or: $80,000 upfront, remainder in two monthly installments of $92,500.

### Recommended Add-Ons (sell separately)

| Add-On | Price | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| SOC 2 audit support (during auditor engagement) | $15,000-25,000 | Liaison and evidence support during the actual Type II audit |
| Penetration testing (if not in base scope) | $20,000-40,000 | External, internal, and/or application pen testing |
| Security training program for staff | $8,000-15,000 | Company-wide security awareness + role-specific training |

Selling add-ons separately keeps the base price competitive while creating expansion revenue. SOC 2 audit support is a natural follow-on that should be mentioned in the proposal.

## Assumptions (Stated Explicitly)

1. **Scope**: "Full security audit" includes network security assessment, application security review, access control review, data protection assessment, and incident response evaluation. It does NOT include full penetration testing unless explicitly scoped (recommend as add-on).

2. **SOC 2 scope**: Preparation for SOC 2 Type II audit, covering the Trust Services Criteria most relevant to fintech (Security, Availability, Confidentiality, and likely Processing Integrity). Privacy may also be in scope given fintech data handling.

3. **Client maturity**: Assumed moderate -- the client has some existing security practices and policies but needs significant work to reach SOC 2 audit-readiness. If the client is starting from near-zero, effort increases 30-50% and timeline may need to extend.

4. **Team size**: Estimated 3-4 consultants at varying commitment levels over 3 months, plus project management and QA review.

5. **Deliverables**: Gap analysis report, remediation roadmap, policies and procedures (created or updated), evidence collection framework, readiness assessment, and training materials. The actual SOC 2 audit (performed by an independent auditor) is NOT included in this engagement.

6. **Technology stack**: Assumed cloud-hosted (AWS, GCP, or Azure) with standard SaaS tools. Unusual or legacy infrastructure would increase complexity and cost.

7. **Client cooperation**: The client will provide timely access to systems, personnel, and documentation. Delays caused by the client may extend the timeline and increase cost.

8. **Your firm's rates**: Internal cost rates used are estimates for a mid-tier specialized cybersecurity firm. Adjust if your actual cost structure is materially different.

## Proposal Strategy Recommendations

1. **Lead with the business problem, not the price.** The proposal should open with the client's situation: they need SOC 2 to close enterprise deals, protect customer data, and strengthen their position for Series C. Frame the engagement as an investment in revenue enablement and risk reduction.

2. **Present three options (Good-Better-Best).** Map to the floor, target, and stretch:
   - **Essential** ($210K): Security audit + SOC 2 gap analysis + remediation roadmap + core policy development. Client handles most remediation internally with your guidance.
   - **Comprehensive** ($265K): Everything in Essential, plus hands-on remediation support, evidence collection framework, pre-audit readiness testing, and staff training. This is the recommended option and should be visually highlighted.
   - **Premium** ($325K): Everything in Comprehensive, plus penetration testing, cloud infrastructure deep-dive, auditor liaison support, and 3 months of post-engagement advisory retainer.

3. **Anchor high.** Present the Premium option first. The Comprehensive option then feels like the reasonable middle ground (compromise effect).

4. **Define scope precisely in the proposal.** List what is included AND what is not included. This protects you from scope creep and sets clear expectations.

5. **Include a timeline with milestones.** Month 1: Discovery and Assessment. Month 2: Remediation and Policy Development. Month 3: Audit Preparation. This shows the client exactly what they are getting and when.

6. **Name the ongoing relationship.** Mention the post-engagement retainer and SOC 2 audit support as natural next steps. This plants the seed for expansion revenue without complicating the initial proposal.

7. **Do not discount to close.** If the client pushes back on price, de-scope rather than discount. Remove deliverables to hit a lower price point rather than doing the same work for less money. This preserves pricing integrity for future engagements.
