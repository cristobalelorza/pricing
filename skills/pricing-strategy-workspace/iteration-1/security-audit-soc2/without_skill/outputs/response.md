# Pricing Recommendation: Full Security Audit + SOC 2 Compliance Preparation
## Client: Series B Fintech Startup ($30M raised, 80 employees)
## Engagement: 3 months

---

## Recommended Price Range

| Tier | Price |
|------|-------|
| **Floor Price** | $150,000 |
| **Target Price** | $220,000 - $260,000 |
| **Stretch Price** | $325,000 |

**Recommended target: $240,000** for the full 3-month engagement.

---

## How I Arrived at This Range

### 1. Cost-Based Analysis (Floor)

A 3-month engagement of this scope typically requires:

- **Lead security consultant / engagement manager**: ~50% dedicated over 3 months
- **Senior security auditor(s)**: 1-2 people, ~75% dedicated
- **Compliance specialist (SOC 2)**: ~60% dedicated
- **Junior analyst / support**: ~50% dedicated

Estimated labor hours: 1,200 - 1,800 billable hours across the team.

At a blended internal cost (fully loaded salary + overhead) of roughly $75-$100/hr for a cybersecurity consulting firm, direct labor cost lands around $90,000 - $180,000. Add operational overhead (tools, licenses, reporting infrastructure, project management, insurance) and a minimum viable margin of 25-35%, and you get a floor around **$150,000**.

Below $150K, you are likely losing money or cutting corners on scope.

### 2. Market-Based Analysis

Market rates for comparable engagements:

- **Standalone comprehensive security audit** for a company this size: $40,000 - $100,000+ depending on depth, number of systems, and whether it includes penetration testing, application security review, cloud infrastructure review, etc.
- **SOC 2 readiness / preparation** (not the actual audit, which is done by a CPA firm): $50,000 - $150,000+ depending on the current state of the client's controls, how much remediation guidance and hands-on implementation support is included.
- **Combined engagements** from established cybersecurity consultancies: $150,000 - $350,000 for a mid-size fintech, depending on scope and firm reputation.

Boutique firms tend to price at the lower half of this range; well-known firms (Big 4 advisory arms, major cybersecurity consultancies) price at the upper half or above.

Since this is a 3-month combined engagement (not two separate projects stitched together), the market midpoint for a credible firm sits around **$200,000 - $280,000**.

### 3. Value-Based Analysis

This is where the price justification gets strongest:

- **SOC 2 compliance is a revenue gate for fintech.** Enterprise customers, banking partners, and regulated entities will require SOC 2 Type II before signing contracts. Failing to achieve compliance delays revenue.
- **A Series B fintech with $30M raised** is almost certainly under pressure to close enterprise deals and demonstrate security maturity to investors, partners, and regulators.
- **The cost of NOT doing this properly** is high: delayed sales cycles (potentially $500K-$2M+ in pipeline delays), failed partner due diligence, or worse, a security incident pre-SOC 2 that damages fundraising and reputation.
- **The cost of doing this poorly** and needing to redo work or failing the actual SOC 2 audit is also significant ($50K-$150K in wasted time and fees).

From a value perspective, if your work helps them close even one or two enterprise deals 3-6 months sooner, the ROI on a $240K engagement is 5-10x or more. This justifies pricing at or above market midpoint.

### 4. Risk and Complexity Adjustments

Factors that push price **up**:
- **Fintech = regulated industry.** Higher stakes, more scrutiny, potentially more complex control environments (PCI overlap, financial data handling, multi-cloud environments).
- **You have not priced this exact engagement before.** First-time scope means higher estimation risk. You should build in a buffer for scope creep and unknowns.
- **80 employees means moderate organizational complexity**: multiple engineering teams, likely a mix of cloud providers, third-party integrations, and varying levels of existing security maturity.
- **3-month timeline is tight** for a full security audit plus SOC 2 readiness if the client is starting from a low maturity baseline. Tight timelines justify premium pricing.

Factors that push price **down**:
- **Series B companies are cost-conscious** but not as price-sensitive as seed-stage. They have budget for this if they see the value.
- **This is a potential long-term relationship**: SOC 2 Type II requires ongoing monitoring, annual re-audits, and continuous compliance. Pricing competitively on the initial engagement can yield recurring revenue.

Net adjustment: **+10-15%** above market midpoint due to fintech complexity, first-time scoping risk, and timeline pressure.

## Recommended Pricing Structure

A flat project fee is not ideal here given the uncertainty. Recommended structure:

### Option A: Fixed Fee with Defined Scope (Recommended)

- **Total engagement fee: $240,000**
- Broken into milestones:
  - **Phase 1 - Security Audit** (Weeks 1-6): $110,000
    - Infrastructure and cloud security assessment
    - Application security review
    - Penetration testing (external + internal)
    - Security architecture review
    - Findings report with prioritized remediation roadmap
  - **Phase 2 - SOC 2 Readiness** (Weeks 5-12, overlapping): $130,000
    - Gap assessment against SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria
    - Policy and procedure development / refinement
    - Control design and implementation guidance
    - Evidence collection framework setup
    - Readiness assessment and pre-audit dry run
    - Remediation support
- Payment terms: 40% at signing, 30% at Phase 1 completion, 30% at Phase 2 completion

**Include a clear scope boundary document.** Define what is in and out (e.g., "remediation guidance" vs. "hands-on implementation of controls" -- the latter costs more and should be scoped carefully or handled via change orders).

### Option B: Base Fee + Time-and-Materials for Remediation Support

- **Base fee: $200,000** covering the audit, gap assessment, policy framework, readiness review, and reporting
- **T&M rate: $275-$325/hr** for hands-on remediation support, implementation assistance, and scope additions
- Cap the T&M at $80,000 unless the client approves an extension
- This protects you if the client's current security posture is weaker than expected

### Option C: Premium / Accelerated Package

If the client needs to be audit-ready in under 3 months or needs additional services (e.g., penetration testing retests, board-ready reporting, vendor security program setup):

- **$300,000 - $325,000** with dedicated senior resources and accelerated timelines

## What I Would Ask the Client Before Finalizing

These unknowns materially affect pricing:

1. **Current security maturity**: Do they have existing security policies, an internal security team, prior audit results, or are they starting from near-zero? Lower maturity = significantly more work.
2. **Scope of systems**: How many applications, cloud environments (AWS/GCP/Azure), third-party integrations, and data stores are in scope?
3. **SOC 2 Type I or Type II readiness?** Type I (point-in-time) is simpler. Type II (over a period, usually 6-12 months) requires setting up ongoing monitoring -- readiness work is more involved.
4. **Is penetration testing included?** If yes, how many targets (external IPs, web apps, internal network segments)?
5. **Remediation scope**: Are you advising on fixes or actually implementing them? Hands-on implementation is a different (and larger) scope.
6. **Existing compliance work**: Any prior SOC 2 attempts, ISO 27001 work, or NIST framework alignment?
7. **Timeline driver**: Is there a specific enterprise deal, investor requirement, or regulatory deadline driving the 3-month window?

---

## Key Assumptions

- The client has at least basic security infrastructure in place (not building from scratch).
- SOC 2 Type I readiness is the primary goal within 3 months; Type II observation period would follow.
- Penetration testing is included at a standard scope (external + internal, 1-2 web applications).
- Your team has the required cybersecurity and compliance expertise in-house (no major subcontracting costs).
- The engagement does not include the actual SOC 2 audit itself (performed by an independent CPA firm).
- Remediation is advisory, not hands-on implementation, unless the T&M option is selected.
