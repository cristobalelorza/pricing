# Precio - Pricing Methodology

## Core Principle
Fair pricing = justified, transparent, and viable for both sides. Not cheapest. Not most expensive.

## Four Lenses
Every pricing recommendation must consider all four lenses. No single lens dominates.

### 1. Cost Lens (Cost Agent)
- Estimate actual delivery costs: labor, materials, tools, overhead
- Add profit margin (default 20-40%)
- Result: price floor (below this = losing money)
- Strongest for: well-scoped, labor-intensive services

### 2. Market Lens (Market Agent)
- Benchmark against competitor and industry rates
- Consider geographic and segment differences
- Result: market-competitive range
- Strongest for: commoditized services with clear comparables
- Warning: do not blindly copy competitors

### 3. Value Lens (Value Agent)
- Estimate business value / ROI for the client
- Price as a fraction of value created (typically 10-30% of estimated value)
- Result: value-based ceiling
- Strongest for: services with measurable business impact
- Warning: only credible when ROI estimate has evidence

### 4. Risk Lens (Risk Agent)
- Assess delivery risk: scope ambiguity, complexity, timeline, dependencies
- Recommend a % adjustment upward
- Typical adjustments: 0-10% low risk, 10-25% moderate, 25-50% high risk
- Applied on top of the arbiter's synthesized price

## Synthesis Rules (Arbiter)
1. Never go below Cost Agent's floor unless explicitly strategic
2. Weight Value Agent higher when credible ROI exists
3. Weight Market Agent higher when service is commoditized
4. Apply Risk Agent's adjustment to final numbers
5. When specialists disagree significantly, explain the conflict and reasoning
6. Recommend pricing structure, not just amount

## Output Format
- **Floor**: minimum viable (covers costs + slim margin + risk)
- **Target**: recommended (balanced across all lenses)
- **Stretch**: aspirational (favorable conditions, premium positioning)
- Always include: rationale, assumptions, risk factors, confidence level

## Validation Rules (Validator)
- Floor must be above breakeven
- Rationale must match the numbers
- Assumptions must be stated, not hidden
- Risk adjustments must be visible
- Structure must fit the service type
- Confidence must reflect available information

## Anti-Patterns
- Single-number pricing without a range
- Hiding uncertainty
- Copying competitors without adjustment
- Ignoring delivery costs
- Pricing below floor without flagging it
- Over-precise numbers from vague inputs (fake precision)
