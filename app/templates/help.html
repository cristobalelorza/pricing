How to Use Pricing

What This App Does

Pricing runs a swarm of 10 AI agents to generate a defensible price recommendation for B2B services. Each agent analyzes your deal from a different angle:

  • Research Agent -- scrapes the client's website to understand their business
  • Cost Agent -- estimates your delivery costs and sets a floor price
  • Market Agent -- benchmarks against what the market charges for comparable work
  • Value Agent -- estimates the ROI and business value for the client
  • Risk Agent -- assesses delivery risks and adjusts the price upward
  • Packaging Agent -- recommends how to structure the pricing (project fee, retainer, phased)
  • Negotiation Agent -- builds a negotiation playbook with tactics and concession strategy
  • Discovery Agent -- generates questions to ask the client to gather more pricing intel
  • Arbiter Agent -- synthesizes all specialist inputs into a final recommendation
  • Validator Agent -- sanity-checks the final output before presenting

Quick Start

  1. Go to Businesses and create a client business (name, URL, location)
  2. Go to Services and create a service template (what you offer, hours, rate)
  3. Go to the business page and click a service to start pricing
  4. Fill in the structured insights (competitor intel, client signals, delivery speed)
  5. Click Get Pricing and wait 30-90 seconds for the agents to run
  6. Review the result: floor / target / stretch prices, negotiation playbook, discovery questions

Understanding the Output

  • Floor -- the minimum price to not lose money (covers costs + slim margin)
  • Target -- the recommended price (balanced across cost, market, value, and risk)
  • Stretch -- the aspirational price (favorable conditions, premium positioning)
  • Confidence -- how confident the system is (higher = more data, clearer scope)

The Negotiation Playbook

The Negotiation Agent produces a strategy for closing the deal:

  • Anchor price -- your opening ask (always higher than target, because the client will negotiate down)
  • Target price -- what you actually want to close at
  • Walk-away price -- the absolute minimum you'll accept
  • Concession ladder -- planned concessions in order, each smaller than the last
  • Objection responses -- how to counter common pushbacks ("too expensive", "we got a lower quote")
  • Tactics -- specific negotiation tactics for this deal (anchoring, bracketing, silence)

Discovery Questions

Use these questions to gather intelligence before or during pricing:

  • Ask the Decision-Maker -- professional questions to ask the client directly
  • Business-Specific -- tailored questions about their industry and situation
  • Indirect Questions -- ask employees or partners who may reveal intel
  • Signals to Listen For -- what their answers reveal about willingness to pay

Structured Insights

The more intel you provide, the better the pricing. Key sections:

  • Your Costs -- hours estimate and hourly rate set the cost floor
  • Delivery Speed -- fast delivery triggers an urgency premium
  • Competitor Intelligence -- if the client uses a similar service, enter the URL and price. We'll auto-compare features
  • Client Signals -- WTP signals, priorities, deal breakers, and budget hints directly influence the price
  • Location -- provider and client location adjust all pricing for local market rates

Tips

  • Fill in Your Location and Client Location -- it dramatically changes the pricing for international deals
  • Use the Re-run with Changes button to see how adding insights changes the price
  • Check the Specialist Agent Details at the bottom of each result to see what each agent individually recommended
  • Use the Feedback widget (? button, bottom-right) to report bugs or suggestions
  • Export results as PDF for client presentations
